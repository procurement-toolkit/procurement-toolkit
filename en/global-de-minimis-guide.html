The Era of Duty-Free Low-Value Imports Is Ending

Since August 2025 in the US, and July 2026 in the EU, major economies have been suspending or phasing out duty-free thresholds for low-value imports. Here's exactly what changed, country by country.

⚠ Key Summary (as of July 2026)

The US effectively ended its $800 de minimis exemption on August 29, 2025 — duties now apply to imports of any value, from any country. The EU began charging a flat €3 duty on parcels under €150 starting July 1, 2026, as an interim step toward fully removing the threshold. South Korea's own de minimis thresholds ($150 / $200 for US-origin goods) remain unchanged by this global trend, for now.

Country-by-Country Status

🇺🇸 United States

Effectively ended

The $800 de minimis exemption was suspended for China and Hong Kong first, effective May 2, 2025, then expanded to all countries of origin effective August 29, 2025. As of now, imports of any value are subject to duty, plus a minimum 10% Section 122 surcharge (set to remain in effect through July 24, 2026, though this could be extended). A June 24, 2026 regulatory change made the suspension effectively permanent, with a statutory repeal reportedly following July 1, 2027.

🇪🇺 European Union

Being phased out

The €150 duty exemption still technically exists, but starting July 1, 2026, parcels under €150 sent directly to consumers now carry a flat €3 customs duty as an interim measure. A separate handling fee is expected to follow by November 1, 2026. VAT has applied to all imports regardless of value since 2021 already.

🇬🇧 United Kingdom

Repeal planned

The £135 duty relief is still in effect today, but the UK government has announced plans to abolish it by October 1, 2028 (moved up six months from the originally planned March 2029 date).

🇯🇵 Japan

Under review

The current threshold is JPY 10,000 (roughly $70–90 USD). Japan is reportedly considering lowering this threshold in response to a surge in cross-border e-commerce parcels. Note that leather goods, knitwear, and certain footwear are excluded from the exemption regardless of value under current rules.

🇨🇦 Canada

Unchanged

Canada maintains one of the lowest thresholds globally at CAD $20. No changes have been reported.

🇦🇺 Australia

Unchanged

The duty-free threshold remains AUD $1,000, but since 2018 overseas sellers above a revenue threshold must collect 10% GST at checkout on goods valued at or under AUD $1,000 — so it isn't fully "duty-free" in practice.

🇰🇷 South Korea

Unchanged

The exemption remains $200 for US-origin goods and $150 for all other countries, unaffected by the global trend so far. Given the broader direction other countries are taking, long-term changes shouldn't be ruled out.

This table reflects the situation as of July 2026. These policies are changing quickly worldwide — for any transaction involving significant value, always verify the current rule with the destination country's customs authority.
What this actually means for you

If you're buying from overseas into the US — assume duty now applies even to small orders under $800. What used to clear duty-free may now come with an unexpected charge.

If you sell into the EU — even small orders now carry customs handling costs. It's a good time to revisit pricing and clearance processes.

If you're sourcing goods for resale or business use — landed cost assumptions built around de minimis thresholds may no longer hold. Re-run your cost calculations with duty included, even for low-value shipments.

Frequently Asked Questions
Does the US de minimis suspension affect shipments into other countries?

No, it only applies to imports into the United States. Each country sets its own de minimis threshold independently.

Is the US de minimis suspension permanent?

A June 2026 regulatory change made the suspension effectively indefinite, with a statutory repeal reportedly following in July 2027. However, trade policy in this area continues to evolve, so nothing should be treated as fully final.

Will South Korea lower its de minimis threshold too?

There's no official announcement of a change as of this writing, but given the broader global trend toward tightening these thresholds, it's a possibility worth watching over the longer term.
