Why Manage FX Exposure

If a large share of your spend sits in one currency, a sharp move in that currency against your reporting currency can push up your costs with little warning. Common responses include diversifying sourcing across currencies, using forward contracts or other hedges, or building exchange-rate clauses into contracts.

How to use this in practice: Review your currency exposure with this tool every quarter. If any single currency crosses 50% of total exposure (you’ll see a concentration warning), that’s a good trigger to discuss hedging options with finance.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is FX exposure the same thing as FX risk?

Exposure is the amount that's affected by exchange rate movements; risk is the likelihood of an actual loss resulting from that exposure. Larger exposure generally means larger risk, so measuring exposure is the first step in managing the risk.

What level of currency concentration is risky?

There's no universal threshold, but as a practical rule of thumb, once a single currency crosses 50% of total purchase exposure, your overall cost becomes heavily dependent on that one currency's movement — a reasonable point to consider diversifying.

Do I need to hedge my FX exposure?

Not necessarily. If your exposure is small or your margins have enough cushion, absorbing the risk without hedging can be a reasonable choice. Either way, you need to measure the exposure first before that decision is even possible.
