How this is calculated:
Sea volumetric weight = CBM × 1,000 kg/m³  |  Air volumetric weight (IATA) = (L×W×H in cm) ÷ 6,000
Chargeable weight = max(actual weight, volumetric weight). Always confirm the exact rate basis with your forwarder — some couriers use a 5,000 divisor instead of 6,000.

Why CBM & Volumetric Weight Matter

Carriers bill whichever is higher: your shipment's actual weight, or its volumetric (dimensional) weight. Light but bulky cargo — think foam displays, inflatables, or bulky packaging — can cost far more than its actual weight suggests once volumetric weight is applied. Estimating CBM before you book helps you avoid surprise freight bills and pick the right container size.

How to use it in practice: Before confirming a booking, enter every carton size in your shipment (mixed cartons are supported) to see total CBM, both sea and air chargeable weight, and how many 20ft/40ft containers you'd need. If air freight chargeable weight is much higher than actual weight, ask your forwarder whether sea freight is more economical for this shipment.
