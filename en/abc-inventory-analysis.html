Frequently Asked Questions
Why use 80%/95% thresholds?

These come from the Pareto Principle (80/20 rule) and are the industry-standard defaults. They're not a fixed rule, though — many companies adjust them to 70%/90% or other splits to fit their situation.

Why does Class A end up with so few items?

That's expected. Typically only 10-20% of SKUs get classified as Class A, and this small group accounting for 70-80% of total consumption value is exactly what a Pareto distribution looks like.

Can I classify by a criterion other than consumption value?

Yes — ABC analysis can also be based on revenue, profit contribution, or the impact of a stockout. The most widely used criterion, though, is annual consumption value (demand × unit cost).
