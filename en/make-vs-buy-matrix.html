What Each Criterion Means

Strategic Importance: How directly this part or capability ties to your product's core competitive advantage.

Internal Capacity: Whether your current equipment and staff can realistically absorb additional production.

Quality Control Requirement: Whether quality variation would have a critical impact on the final product.

IP Protection Need: Whether outsourcing risks leaking core technology or design.

Supply Market Maturity: Whether enough reliable suppliers exist in the market (more suppliers favors Buy).

Frequently Asked Questions
What if the cost comparison and strategic scoring disagree?

This happens often in practice. If cost favors Buy but strategic importance is very high, teams frequently choose Make anyway, prioritizing long-term competitiveness over short-term cost. A disagreement between the two is a signal to discuss carefully, not a bug.

How is the breakeven volume calculated?

It's the volume at which both options' total cost (fixed + variable × volume) becomes equal. Above that volume, the option with higher fixed cost (usually Make) becomes cheaper; below it, the other option wins.

How should I set the weights?

There's no fixed answer. The common practice is to make the five weights sum to 100% and reflect your organization's priorities — for example, weighting IP protection higher in an industry where that matters most.
