How to Use This Calendar

The "practical impact window" is almost always much longer than the official holiday. Chinese New Year, for example, is an 8-day official holiday, but it actually takes 4-6 weeks for factory output to fully normalize. This calendar calculates the "recommended order-by date" based on that practical window, not just the public holiday dates.

Ordering after the recommended date doesn't mean you won't receive your goods — it's a signal that your supplier lead time is likely to run much longer than usual.

Frequently Asked Questions
How is the "recommended order-by date" calculated?

It's based on when each event's practical supply chain impact actually begins, which is often 1-3 weeks earlier than the official holiday. For an exact lead time, always confirm directly with your supplier or carrier.

Why doesn't this include every country's public holidays?

This calendar only includes events that cause a genuinely significant supply chain disruption. Rather than every one-day holiday, it focuses on events like Chinese New Year and Golden Week that halt logistics for days or weeks at a time.

Does this include dates beyond 2027?

Major events with an already-confirmed date, like the next Chinese New Year (February 2027), are included. However, since most governments only officially announce next year's holidays in the second half of the prior year, earlier estimates will be updated once confirmed.
