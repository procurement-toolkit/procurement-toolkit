Why Calculate Lead Time in Advance

Quoting a delivery date without adding up every stage — production, inland transport, ocean/air transit, and customs — often leads to missed promises or unplanned stockouts.

How to use it in practice: Calculate the expected arrival date before confirming a new order, and recalculate with a larger buffer during peak shipping season when transit times typically increase.

Frequently Asked Questions
What stages actually make up total lead time?

Total lead time typically includes production lead time (order to production completion), sea or air transit time, customs clearance time, and domestic delivery time. Estimating arrival based on production time alone usually results in a date that's too optimistic.

How long does customs clearance typically take?

With complete documentation and no special inspection required, clearance often takes just 1-3 days. But items requiring quarantine, safety certification, or other special review can take significantly longer, so it's safer to build in extra buffer time.
