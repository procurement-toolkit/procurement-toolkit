Key Terms

BATNA: Your best alternative if this negotiation fails. A stronger BATNA gives you more negotiating power.

Reservation Price: The point beyond which you're better off walking away and taking your BATNA instead.

ZOPA: The overlap between your reservation price and the counterparty's reservation price. A deal is only structurally possible if this overlap exists.

Frequently Asked Questions
How do I estimate the counterparty's range?

Base it on their cost structure, market pricing, past deal history, and competitor quotes. It doesn't need to be precise — even a rough range meaningfully improves your negotiation strategy.

If there's no ZOPA, should I give up on the negotiation?

Not necessarily — first consider whether adjusting non-price terms (lead time, payment terms, volume, warranty) could widen the ZOPA. Bundling multiple issues together can create room for a deal that pure price negotiation couldn't.

Why should my reservation price be based on my BATNA?

The worst deal you should accept can never be worse than your best alternative (BATNA) if the negotiation fails. If a deal is worse than your BATNA, walking away is the rational choice.
