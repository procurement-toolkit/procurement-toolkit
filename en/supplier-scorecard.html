Frequently Asked Questions
What categories should a supplier evaluation cover?

Common categories are quality (defect rate), on-time delivery performance, price competitiveness, responsiveness/communication, and financial stability. Evaluating on price alone tends to miss significant operational risk.

How often should new vs. established suppliers be reviewed?

New suppliers are often reviewed quarterly during the first year, then shifted to a semi-annual or annual cadence once performance stabilizes. Paying close attention to the first two or three orders is especially valuable for catching quality or delivery issues early.
