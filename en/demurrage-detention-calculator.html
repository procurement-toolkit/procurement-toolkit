Frequently Asked Questions
What's the difference between demurrage and detention?

Demurrage is the storage fee charged while a container stays inside the port terminal, while detention is charged for using the container outside the terminal, up until the empty is returned to the carrier. Both are billed per day once free time expires.

Why does the rate get more expensive the longer it goes?

Carriers and terminals want to keep their equipment circulating quickly, so the rate structure is designed to penalize holding a container longer. The first few days are relatively cheap, but the rate often steps up sharply after that.

How can I reduce these costs?

The most reliable approach is locking in your customs clearance and unloading schedule early enough to finish within free time. If a delay looks likely, requesting a free time extension from the carrier or forwarder in advance is another option.
