Frequently Asked Questions
How is the Standard Price set?

It's usually based on last year's average purchase price, market pricing at the time the budget was set, or a negotiated contract price with the supplier. Most companies already have this figure recorded in their ERP or budgeting system.

Does a positive PPV mean poor buyer performance?

Not necessarily. External factors buyers can't control — raw material price spikes, exchange rate shifts, supplier changes — can also drive PPV up. That said, a large, recurring variance is still worth investigating.

Are PPV and cost savings the same thing?

No. PPV is an accounting measure of budget-vs-actual, while cost savings measures the actual dollar amount saved compared to before. In a year when raw material prices spike, PPV can look unfavorable even though negotiating a smaller increase than the market average represents real cost avoidance.
