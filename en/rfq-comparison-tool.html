Frequently Asked Questions
What if the lowest-price supplier and the highest-scoring supplier differ?

This happens often. If the cheapest option has a very long lead time or an uncertain quality track record, choosing the higher-scoring option despite a somewhat higher price can be the better long-term call. This tool exists to document exactly that rationale.

How do I score quality and reliability?

There's no fixed formula. Combine past dealings, sample quality, certifications held, quote response speed, and communication into a 1 (poor) to 5 (excellent) rating.

How should I set the weights?

It depends on what you're buying. For standard, commoditized items with little quality variation, weight price more heavily; for precision parts or a new, unproven supplier, weight quality and reliability more heavily.
