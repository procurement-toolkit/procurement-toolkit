Why a Dedicated Template Matters

Generic invoice templates often miss fields that trade actually requires — Incoterm, country of origin, HS Code — forcing you to rebuild the format every time. Errors on shipping documents can delay customs clearance.

How to use it in practice: Generate both the invoice and packing list from a single item entry for every shipment, and record special cases — samples, dangerous goods — in the remarks field for customs reference.

Frequently Asked Questions
What's the difference between a commercial invoice and a packing list?

A commercial invoice is the commercial document that proves the transaction value and terms — customs uses it as the basis for duty assessment. A packing list documents the physical details of the shipment (packaging units, weight, dimensions). Customs typically cross-checks both documents together.

What fields are essential on a commercial invoice?

Exporter and importer details, the Incoterm used, item description and HS code, quantity, unit price, total value, and country of origin are the core fields. Missing any of these is a common cause of customs clearance delays.
