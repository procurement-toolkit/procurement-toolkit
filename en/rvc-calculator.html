Frequently Asked Questions
Should I use Build-Up or Build-Down?

Most FTAs let you choose either method. If you know your originating material content precisely, Build-Up is more convenient; if you only track non-originating material costs, Build-Down is easier. Some agreements do restrict which method is allowed for certain products, so this is worth confirming.

Is Adjusted Value the same as the sale price?

It's usually based on the exporter's transaction value (e.g., FOB price), but what's included or excluded (freight, commissions, etc.) can vary by agreement. Check the specific "value calculation" rule in the relevant FTA text for the exact definition.

What happens if I don't meet the RVC threshold?

The standard (MFN) tariff rate applies instead of the preferential rate. You could consider shifting part of your material sourcing to originating suppliers, or checking whether an alternative origin test (such as a tariff classification shift rule) might apply instead.
