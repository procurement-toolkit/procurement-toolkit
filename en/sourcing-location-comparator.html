Don't Miss These Hidden Costs

Longer lead times mean higher inventory cost. Even if offshore has a lower unit price, a longer lead time means holding more safety stock, which raises your inventory carrying cost. The "total cost" in this calculator only covers materials, freight, and duty — inventory cost should be calculated separately for an accurate picture.

Currency risk also varies by sourcing location. Offshore sourcing tends to carry greater FX exposure.

Frequently Asked Questions
Why aren't average labor cost and freight figures pre-filled by country?

These figures vary hugely by timing, exchange rate, and exact region, and change quickly. Pre-filling rough statistics risks leading you to a wrong decision based on numbers that don't match reality, so this tool asks you to enter quotes you've verified yourself.

What's the difference between nearshoring and reshoring?

Reshoring means bringing production back to your home country. Nearshoring means moving it to a geographically closer country that isn't your home country (e.g., a US company moving production to Mexico). Both are primarily motivated by shorter lead times and risk diversification.

Why choose nearshore/reshore even at a higher unit price?

Factors beyond unit price — lower inventory cost from shorter lead times, reduced FX risk, easier quality control, and geopolitical risk diversification — can meaningfully affect total cost. See the "Hidden Costs" section of this calculator.
