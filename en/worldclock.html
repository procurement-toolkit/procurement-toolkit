Why time zone awareness matters

When you're scheduling a call or video meeting with an overseas partner, it's easy to miscalculate the time difference and end up reaching out in the middle of their night. This mistake gets more common the more countries you deal with at once.

How to use this: Check a trade partner's current local time and whether they're within business hours before scheduling a meeting, or use it to find an overlapping window when coordinating with contacts across several regions.

Frequently Asked Questions
How do I find a good time to call an overseas partner?

Look for the overlap between both parties' typical business hours (usually 09:00-18:00 local). For large time zone gaps (e.g., Korea and the US East Coast), the overlap may be very short or nonexistent, in which case email tends to work better than trying to schedule a live call.

Does the business-hours badge account for public holidays?

No — it's a simple estimate based on weekday and time only (09:00-18:00 local). It doesn't account for local public holidays or company-specific working hours, so double-check before an important meeting.
