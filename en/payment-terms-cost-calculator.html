Frequently Asked Questions
How is the annualized cost calculated?

It uses the industry-standard NACM formula: [Discount % ÷ (100 − Discount %)] × [360 ÷ (Full Term − Discount Period)]. For 2/10 net 30, that works out to [2/98] × [360/20] ≈ 36.7%.

Why does the number come out so high (like 36.7%)?

A 2% discount seems small, but it applies to just 20 days (the gap between the discount period and the full term), so annualizing it multiplies the effect substantially. Discounts concentrated in a short window naturally produce large annualized figures — that's expected.

If the annualized cost is higher than my cost of capital, should I always take the discount?

Generally yes — delaying payment would effectively mean paying "interest" more expensive than your own borrowing cost. That said, if cash is genuinely tight right now, you may not have the funds to take the discount even when it's theoretically favorable.
